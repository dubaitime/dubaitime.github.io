## Dubai Bus Fares

Dubai’s fare system is based on seven zones, with charges varying according to the distance traveled or number of zones crossed. Transfers between buses, metro, trams, and water buses within 30 minutes are charged as a single trip. Beyond this time frame, separate fares apply.

Fare Structure Using NOL Cards:

| Fare according to Zone | Silver Card | Gold Card | Blue Personal Card | Red Card (Ticket) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Within 1 Zone | AED 3 | AED 6 | AED 3 | AED 4 |
| 2 Adjacent Zones | AED 5 | AED 10 | AED 5 | AED 6 |
| More than 2 Zones | AED 7.5 | AED 15 | AED 7.5 | AED 8.5 |

Discounts are available for students, senior citizens, and residents with blue personalized cards. People of Determination and children under 5 travel for free. Fines are imposed for violations such as using invalid or expired cards, with a penalty of AED 200 or AED 500 for fake cards.

## General Guidelines on Using Public Buses in Dubai

- Reserved Seating: Three rows behind the driver are for ladies and children.

- Prohibitions: Eating, drinking, smoking, and creating noise are not allowed. Animals and heavy luggage are also prohibited.

## Comparison of RTA Dubai Bus Route Types

| Bus Route Type | Purpose | Coverage Area | Key Characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|
| RTA Dubai Express Bus Routes | Fast and direct connections to major destinations | Major hubs and key locations within Dubai | Fewer stops; faster travel time; prefixed with "E" |
| RTA Dubai Feeder Bus Routes | Connects residential areas to main metro stations | Residential neighborhoods and metro stations | Smaller, local routes feeding into larger transport systems |
| RTA Urban CBD Bus Routes | Serves the Central Business District (CBD) | Central business areas of Dubai | High frequency; caters to office commuters |
| RTA Urban Bus Routes | General city transportation | Various city areas across Dubai | Extensive network; connects different neighborhoods and commercial areas |
| RTA Intercity Routes | Links Dubai to neighboring cities | Routes to cities like Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, Ajman, etc. | Longer routes; prefixed with "E" for intercity travel |

Feeder bus routes are essential for enhancing connectivity between metro stations and various neighborhoods in Dubai. By using these routes, commuters can efficiently transfer between different parts of the city, optimizing their travel experience. Understanding the feeder bus network will help you navigate Dubai's public transport system with ease, ensuring a smooth and integrated journey across the Emirate.

Abu Dhabi’s Bus 380 is a convenient transportation route connecting Al Ain Bus Station to Al Faqa, ending at the Al Rahman Mosque. Whether you're heading for work, visiting a mosque, or exploring the city’s outskirts, Bus 380 provides a comfortable and reliable travel option.

## Operational Details

Bus 380 operates four times a day, both from Al Ain and Al Faqa. Below are the timings for Bus 380:

| From Al Ain | From Al Faqa (Al Rahman Mosque) |
|---|---|
| 06:00 | 08:00 |
| 10:00 | 12:00 |
| 14:00 | 16:00 |
| 18:00 | 20:00 |

Bus 380 Timetable

To view the full schedule, you can visit the official AD Mobility website or follow this link for the 380 Bus Timings.

Ticket Pricing: Payments for Bus 380 are made using the Hafilat Card, Abu Dhabi's cashless payment system. This card ensures seamless travel across all buses. The base fare is AED 2, with additional charges depending on the distance traveled. Passengers can top up their Hafilat cards online or at kiosks located throughout the city.

## Accessibility and Services

The buses feature:

- Ramps for wheelchair access.

- Designated seating for individuals requiring assistance.
- Audio-visual aids to assist passengers with hearing or vision impairments.

This makes Bus 380 an inclusive choice for all commuters.

## Nearby Attractions and Landmarks
1. Hili Fun City  One of Al Ain’s oldest amusement parks, offering fun rides for families and tourists.

2. Hazza bin Zayed Stadium  A state-of-the-art sports complex where football matches and events are regularly held.

3. Al Jimi Mall  A premier shopping destination in Al Ain, housing international brands and diverse dining options.

These attractions make the journey on Bus 380 not just a commute but an opportunity to explore Al Ain's vibrant culture and recreational facilities.

## Journey Experience

On a recent trip on Bus 380 from Al Ain to Al Faqa, travelers enjoyed a scenic blend of urban and rural landscapes. Along the way, passengers caught glimpses of Hili Fun City, an amusement park perfect for families.

The route meandered through peaceful agricultural areas, showcasing Al Ain’s rich farming heritage. Finally, the bus stopped at Al Rahman Mosque in Al Faqa, a serene community centerpiece. Overall, the journey provided a unique opportunity to explore the culture and attractions of Al Ain. It's a must-try for anyone looking to experience the local charm.

## Travel Tips for Bus 380

- Plan Ahead: With only four trips a day, make sure to check the bus schedule to avoid long waits.

- Use the Hafilat Card: This makes fare payments quick and hassle-free, ensuring smooth boarding.
- Monitor Bus Updates: Stay informed about real-time changes in the bus schedule by using the AD Mobility app.
- Explore Attractions: Take time to visit nearby landmarks like Hili Fun City or Al Jimi Mall to make the most of your trip.

If you're looking for a reliable, affordable, and straightforward way to get from Dubai to Sharjah, this guide provides detailed insights into bus routes, fares, and schedules. Whether for work or leisure, you’ll find practical tips on how to make your journey hassle-free.

## 1. Line 107 Bus - Best Option

- Route: Global Village 1 (Dubai) to Sahara Center (Sharjah)

- Estimated Travel Time: 1 hour 35 minutes
- Fare: AED 1720
- Frequency: Every 3 hours

Journey Overview:

Step 1: If you’re near the Global Village area, simply head to the nearest bus stop. The 107 bus route begins from this popular spot and is easy to catch for travelers in the area.

Step 2: Board the 107 bus at Global Village. The bus operates every three hours and stops at key locations along the way.

- Key Stops: Rebat Street, Al Nahda 1, Terminus 2

- Duration: Approximately 1 hour from Global Village to Sahara Center

Step 3: The bus will drop you off near Sahara Center, allowing easy access to various destinations in Sharjah. From here, you can walk about 700 meters to the Al Nahda 1 terminus or take additional local buses as needed.

## 2. Line 103 and E303 Buses - Alternate Option

If you are starting from the Union Metro Station area in Dubai, the Line 103 bus combined with the E303 bus can also take you to Sharjah. This option involves a transfer but covers more stops for those who need additional flexibility.

- Route: Union Metro Station (Deira, Dubai) to Al Jubail Bus Station (Sharjah)

- Estimated Travel Time: 1 hour 51 minutes
- Fare: AED 1320

Journey Overview:

Step 1: Begin at the Union Metro Station in Dubai, located in the Deira area. Head to Union Square Bus Station, a short walk from the metro, to find the 103 bus.

Step 2: Board the 103 bus at Union Square Bus Station, which will take you to Rebat Street and other intermediate stops. The 103 bus is direct and runs every hour.

- Key Stops: Union Square, Rebat Street, Al Karama

Step 3: At Union Square Bus Station, transfer to the E303 bus bound for Sharjah, Al Jubail Bus Station.

- Key Stops: Salah Al Din Metro, Reef Mall, Abu Hail, Sharjah Mamzar, Al Wahda Center

Step 4: The E303 bus will take you directly to Sharjah’s Al Jubail Bus Station, offering convenient access to popular areas in Sharjah.

## Travel Tips

- Plan Your Departure: Buses run on fixed schedules, so planning your trip in advance will help you avoid long waiting times, especially for the 107 bus, which runs every three hours.

- Avoid Peak Times: Dubai and Sharjah buses can get crowded during rush hours (typically 5 PM to 7 PM). Travel outside of these times if possible for a more comfortable ride.
- Check Real-Time Schedules: Use the RTA website or app to get real-time updates on bus timings and availability, ensuring you won’t miss your bus.
- Nearby Attractions: If time permits, explore Deira’s famous Gold Souk or Spice Souk before departing from Union Metro Station or visit Sahara Center’s shopping complex once you arrive in Sharjah.

Some other interesting trips you can explore are from Sharjah to Dubai and the trip from Dubai to Al Ain.

Traveling from Dubai to Sharjah by bus is a straightforward and budget-friendly option. Whether you take the direct Line 107 bus from Global Village or combine the 103 and E303 lines from Union Metro, both options offer comfortable and reliable transit. With this guide, you’ll be well-prepared to enjoy a smooth journey across two of the UAE’s most vibrant cities. Safe travels!
